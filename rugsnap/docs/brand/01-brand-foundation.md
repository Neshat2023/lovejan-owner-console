# 01 — Brand Foundation

**Date:** 2026-07-23 · **Status:** Proposed foundation for the parent brand. RugSnap remains the live product brand; nothing here changes production.

## What we understand about the business (stated per mandate)

RugSnap (LoveJan, Inc.) is a live product: photograph a valuable → AI identifies and values it → one tap lists it on a marketplace with protected payment (Stripe Connect, delayed seller payout), cards + Apple Pay + BNPL. Deployed on Vercel, source on GitHub, domains rugsnap.com/.app owned. The long-term opportunity spans rugs, art, antiques, watches, jewelry, coins, collectibles, furniture, vintage/designer objects. Core loop: SCAN → IDENTIFY → UNDERSTAND → VALUE → VERIFY → LIST → SELL → PAY → SHIP.

**Major strategic decisions to resolve:** (1) brand architecture (A/B/C/D below in doc 02); (2) niche-vs-general sequencing; (3) whether a parent name is needed now and what it is; (4) what happens to RugSnap.

**Research constraints declared up front:** from this environment, live verification is possible **only** for domain availability/pricing (via the Vercel registrar API, timestamped in doc 04). **Assumptions:** solo-operated company near-term; capital-light; US-first with worldwide buyers; Farsi/English bilingual identity matters; owner holds Neshat.AI domain (treated as unverified for trademark like every other candidate).

## Foundation (proposed)

1. **Mission:** remove fear from owning and trading valuable physical objects.
2. **Ten-year vision:** the default trust layer for physical valuables — where objects are identified, valued, verified, and traded with confidence.
3. **Brand purpose:** people deserve to know what they own — and to trade it without being cheated.
4. **Category definition (recommended language):** **"Trusted commerce platform for physical valuables, powered by AI appraisal."** Rationale: "AI appraisal platform" undersells commerce; "intelligent marketplace" undersells trust; "operating system for objects" is investor-speak consumers don't feel. Lead with trust+commerce, let AI be the how.
5. **Primary customers:** owners of valuables (esp. inheritors/estate executors), collectors, buyers of secondhand valuables, dealers (supply partners), later insurers/appraisers (B2B).
6. **Customer problem:** "I don't know what this is worth, whether it's real, or how to sell/buy it without being cheated."
7. **UVP:** a 30-second AI appraisal that becomes a permanent item record and a protected transaction.
8. **Competitive position:** more trusted than classifieds (eBay/FB), faster and cheaper than auction houses and human appraisers, deeper than visual search (Google Lens), transactional where price databases (WorthPoint) stop. *(Landscape from model knowledge — UNVERIFIED currency.)*
9. **Emotional promise:** relief — "now I know, and I'm safe."
10. **Functional promise:** identify, value, verify, sell, get paid — one flow.
11. **Personality:** knowledgeable, calm, honest about uncertainty, warmly premium — an expert friend, not an auctioneer.
12. **Voice:** plain language; ranges not false precision; never hype; bilingual grace (EN/FA).
13. **Trust principles:** show confidence honestly (abstain when unsure); never blur AI-assessed with authenticated; protect money until delivery is confirmed; privacy by default on evidence.
14. **Values:** honesty about uncertainty, craft respect, fairness to both sides, cultural rootedness without exclusivity.
15. **Strategic differentiators:** appraisal-backed listings, item records (passports), three-axis trust, outcome-corrected valuations.
16. **Long-term data advantage:** estimate-vs-realized price per category — accumulates only to us.
17. **Network effect:** every sale transfers an item record to a new user; every outcome improves every future appraisal.
18. **Moat:** outcome dataset + trust brand + supply relationships (dealers/appraisers).
19. **Never become:** a pawn-shop vibe, a hype auction casino, a data-exploitative tracker, or a brand that fakes certainty.
20. **Five-second message:** "Know what it's worth. Sell it safely."
