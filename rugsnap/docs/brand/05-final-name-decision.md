## Five finalists — condensed dossiers

**1. VELUMA** — *coined: value + lumen (light on value)*
Story: "we shine light on what things are worth." Trust: sounds calm, premium, non-hype; works for a bank-grade trust brand and a consumer app equally. AI-fit: abstract enough to cover identification→valuation→commerce. Covers all categories: yes — no object bias. RugSnap fit: "RugSnap by Veluma" endorsement reads naturally. Taglines: "Know what it's worth." / "Every object, understood." Product system: Veluma Appraisal, Veluma Passport, Veluma Bazaar (rugs). Domain plan: veluma.com registered (owner UNVERIFIED) → broker inquiry at trigger; defensive: valuma/velumah + .ai/.app (also held — acquire or ignore). Biggest hidden risk: a quiet existing user of the name surfacing in commerce classes. Best case: category-defining trust brand. Worst case: TM conflict forces a late rename — mitigated by clearing *before* adoption.

**2. VITRINE** — *the case where valuables are displayed*
Story: "your valuables, in the light, behind glass — seen and protected." Uniquely bilingual: an everyday Farsi loanword (ویترین) and a natural French/Turkish/Portuguese word — the only candidate our community already says daily. Trust: museum-grade connotation; premium without elitism. Risks: EN pronunciation teaching ("vih-TREEN"), genericness limits TM strength, heavy generic SEO noise in FR/PT. RugSnap fit: "RugSnap, part of Vitrine." Best case: beloved culturally-rooted brand. Worst case: unprotectable mark + pronunciation friction in the US core market.

**3. VALYT** — *value + vault*
The only finalist with a **verified acquirable domain today** (valyt.ai, $160/2yr, checked 2026-07-23). Story: "your value, vaulted." Trust: fintech-grade sound. Risks: FA/AR homophone ولایت needs native review; spelling must be taught; .ai-primary weakens type-in trust for commerce. Best case: crisp modern trust brand acquired for lunch money. Worst case: forever explaining the spelling while the .com owner squats.

**4. AESTIMA** — *Latin aestimare, to appraise*
Story: the classical root of "estimate" — we are the appraisal company, etymologically. Premium, credible, global Latin. Risks: users type "estima" (Toyota model noise), spelled≠heard, both .coms held. Best case: authoritative institution-feel brand (fits insurance/B2B era). Worst case: perpetual spelling tax.

**5. APPRA** — *appraisal, truncated to a verb*
Story: "just appra it" — the only candidate with new-category-verb potential. Risks: appra.com/.ai/.app all held (likely appraisal-tech neighbors — direct-space conflict risk), FA اپرا/opera collision. Best case: the verb of the category. Worst case: cease-and-desist from an existing appraisal startup.

## Final recommendation

1. **Primary:** **Veluma** — conditional on counsel clearance + .com acquisition feasibility within budget D6.
2. **Backup:** **Aestima** — if Veluma fails clearance/acquisition. (Vitrine drops to third: cultural brilliance, legal/SEO fragility.)
3. **Lower-cost alternative:** **Valyt** with valyt.ai ($160, verified) — acceptable only with FA-homophone sign-off and a standing .com acquisition intent.
4. **RugSnap:** **keep** — as the rugs wedge product brand, later endorsed ("RugSnap by <Parent>"); never extend the Snap pattern to new categories; include RugSnap in the counsel scope (Snap Inc. adjacency, preliminary/UNVERIFIED).
5. **Architecture:** endorsed hybrid → single universal platform (doc 02, Option D→C).
6. **Domain portfolio:** keep rugsnap.com/.app; acquire parent .com + .ai/.app + 1–2 misspellings at trigger; buy nothing today.
7. **Why this wins:** Veluma is the only candidate scoring high on distinctiveness, category breadth, premium trust, and multilingual safety simultaneously; every alternative carries a structural flaw (genericness, spelling tax, verified crowding, or homophone risk) rather than a merely unverified one.
8. **Strongest argument against it:** it means nothing yet — we must buy every ounce of meaning with marketing, while Vitrine arrives pre-loaded with meaning for our core community. If brand budget will be ~zero for years, Vitrine's built-in meaning may beat Veluma's blank slate.
9. **I change this recommendation if:** counsel finds a commerce-class conflict; the veluma.com owner demands a price >D6 ceiling; native FA/AR review flags Valyt as clean AND budget stays minimal (then Valyt rises); or a vertical-#2 pivot makes the estate journey dominant (then Heirly-type estate branding re-enters).
10. **Confidence:** 68/100, moderate — capped by unverified trademark reality, honestly.
11. **Exact next verification steps before registering anything:** (1) USPTO TESS + WIPO + EUIPO knockout search on Veluma/Aestima/Valyt in classes 9, 35, 36, 42, 45; (2) WHOIS/RDAP + broker inquiry on veluma.com and aestima.com; (3) native-speaker FA/AR/ES/DE/CJK review; (4) App Store/Play/handle sweep; (5) counsel clearance opinion; (6) only then, D6-budgeted acquisition.
12. **30-day plan (trigger-independent prep):** week 1 — owner sets D6 budget; week 2 — counsel engaged for knockout search (bundled with the ToS work in v1.1 plan D7); week 3 — broker inquiries (no offers); week 4 — decision memo update to this doc with verified data, then stop until the Bible trigger fires.
